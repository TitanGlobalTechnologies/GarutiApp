Here's the real talk on Naples real estate nobody wants to say out loud.

In 2019 you needed about $66k household income to comfortably buy here. Fast forward to today and the average Naples home is pushing $600k plus. Your mortgage alone is running $4,500 to $5,000 a month. To stay at a healthy debt to income ratio you're looking at needing $150k household income minimum.

Most people aren't there and that's totally fine.

What's actually happening right now is prices are softening back toward 2021 and 2022 levels. Inventory is up. Competition has shifted in favor of buyers for the first time in years. If you bought at peak pricing in 2022 or 2023 you're probably flat right now and that's okay because real estate is a long game.

Naples isn't going anywhere. The demand is real. The timing just matters more than ever.
