Naples home prices are shifting and everyone's asking if a crash is coming. Here's my honest take.

Single family prices are dropping year over year across most of Florida right now. But Naples is a totally different story. We've seen massive appreciation since 2020 and the demand here from out of state buyers and seasonal residents keeps this market propped up in ways other cities just don't have.

Look at Cape Coral down 17%. Port Charlotte down 22%. Meanwhile Naples and the surrounding areas held strong because of who's buying here. High net worth buyers paying cash don't disappear when interest rates move.

The crash crew has been screaming since 2020. They've been wrong every single year. Is inflation suddenly going to drop 30 percent? Probably not. Prices staying elevated makes complete sense.
