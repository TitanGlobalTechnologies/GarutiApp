Cape Coral home prices hit around $400k average right now. But the median household income here is roughly $77k. To stay at a healthy 40% debt to income ratio you actually need closer to $108k to comfortably afford that. Most people aren't there yet and that's totally okay.

Here's the thing though. Back in 2019 Cape Coral homes were averaging around $265k and incomes lined up way better with prices. That math made sense. Right now it doesn't for a lot of buyers.

What I'm actually seeing sell in Cape Coral today are homes priced back at 2020 or 2022 levels. Insurance costs are up. Property taxes are up. The rental market is oversaturated with inventory. It's genuinely tough out there.

But if you're planning to buy in 2026 or 2027 this is actually your time to prep and position yourself right.
