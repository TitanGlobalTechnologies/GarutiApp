These walls can't talk. If they did they would have a lot to tell us. This house was built in 1907 by Frederick Miller from a well-known Fort Myers name, John Morgan Dean. Dean was one of Fort Myers' first real estate developers. He didn't just put in houses, he put in sidewalks and shrubs and made it a neighborhood that people wanted to call home. This area was known as Millionaires' Road because prominent businessmen and professionals came here to build stately homes and mansions, some of which still remain. There are three floors of living. The main level features two separate seating areas, a spacious dining kitchen, a cigar room, and office space overlooking the yard. The second floor features an ensuite with full bath, a guest bath, and two more bedrooms. And the third floor is a bonus space perfect for fun and creativity. You can see why people would want to make their home here. It's right on first street. The Kallusa Hatchee River is right over the sight line and downtown is within walking distance. So this was a great place to live over a hundred years ago and it's still a great place to live today.
